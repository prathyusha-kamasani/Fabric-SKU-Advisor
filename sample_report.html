Key Metrics

These KPIs summarise your capacity health at a glance.

  • Avg Utilisation — average daily smoothed CU consumption as a percentage of SKU budget
  • P80 Daily CUs — 80th-percentile daily consumption (the “typical busy day”)
  • Peak Utilisation — highest single-day utilisation observed
  • Burst Ratio — peak-to-average ratio. Higher values indicate spiky workloads

Capacity Intelligence

These insights are generated from your data using pattern recognition — no AI involved. Each card identifies a specific pattern in your capacity metrics and suggests an action.

  • Warning cards indicate issues that need attention
  • Info cards highlight useful observations
  • Success cards confirm healthy patterns

Consumption Analysis

Charts and statistics showing how your capacity is consumed over time.

  • Daily utilisation shows the trend over the analysis period
  • Weekday vs weekend helps identify scheduling opportunities
  • Spike filtering excludes anomalous days (e.g. pause/resume settlement) from sizing

Workload Breakdown

Analysis of which items and types consume the most capacity.

  • Top consumers identifies the items driving CU usage
  • Item type distribution shows the workload mix
  • Hourly patterns reveal scheduling opportunities

Weekday vs Weekend

Compares consumption patterns between weekdays and weekends to identify scheduling opportunities.

  • Ratio > 1.5x means weekday-heavy workloads — typical for business reporting
  • Ratio near 1.0 means similar load 7 days/week — consider whether weekend refreshes are needed

Spike Filtering

Days with abnormally high consumption (e.g. pause/resume settlement spikes, one-off migrations) are detected and excluded from the P80 sizing calculation.

This prevents anomalous days from inflating your SKU recommendation.

Reserved vs PAYG Pricing

Fabric capacity can be purchased on a pay-as-you-go (hourly) basis or with a 1-year reservation for significant savings.

  • PAYG — flexible, no commitment, higher per-hour cost
  • Reserved — 1-year commitment, typically 40-50% cheaper

Prices shown are approximate USD monthly costs based on published Azure pricing.

Recommendations

Three options are presented to give you flexibility:

  • Option A (Scale) — change SKU size to match demand
  • Option B (Optimise) — reschedule workloads to reduce peak contention
  • Option C (Combined) — moderate scale change plus scheduling improvements

The top consumers table shows which items to prioritise for scheduling changes. Urgency level is based on health score and throttling severity.

Cost & Recommendations

SKU sizing uses the 80/80 method: P80 daily consumption at 80% target utilisation, leaving 20% headroom.

  • Option A — pure scaling (change SKU)
  • Option B — optimise scheduling (move workloads to off-peak)
  • Option C — combined approach

Reserved pricing assumes a 1-year commitment. PAYG = pay-as-you-go (no commitment).
